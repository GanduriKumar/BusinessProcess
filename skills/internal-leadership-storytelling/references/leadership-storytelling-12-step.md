# Leadership Storytelling 12-Step Guide

Use this as the source-of-truth question set for internal leadership storytelling.

## 1. Real delivery challenge

- What real delivery challenge in current engagements does this solution address?
- What operational pain do delivery teams face today?
- How frequently does this issue occur across engagements?
- What risks or inefficiencies does it create?

## 2. Why now

- Why is solving this problem important now?
- What risks exist if the current situation continues?
- How does this impact cost, quality, delivery timelines, or customer satisfaction?
- Is this challenge becoming more significant with scale or new technologies?

## 3. Proposed solution

- How does the proposed solution address this challenge?
- What is the core idea behind the solution?
- How does it work at a high level?
- Which part of the delivery lifecycle does it improve?

## 4. Solution maturity and reliability

- How mature and reliable is the solution?
- What stage is the solution currently in: concept, prototype, pilot, or production?
- Has it been tested in real delivery scenarios?
- What evidence exists to demonstrate its effectiveness?

## 5. Measurable benefits

- What measurable benefits does the solution deliver?
- What improvements can be expected in productivity, quality, or risk reduction?
- How does it impact delivery cost or efficiency?
- What measurable outcomes have been observed or projected?

## 6. Adoption and deployment

- How easy is it for delivery teams to adopt and deploy the solution?
- What is the lead time required for deployment?
- What changes are required in existing workflows?
- How quickly can teams begin realizing value?

## 7. Dependencies and prerequisites

- What dependencies or prerequisites are required?
- What tools, platforms, or integrations are required?
- Are there skill or training requirements?
- How easily can these dependencies be addressed?

## 8. Alternatives and comparison

- What alternatives exist and how does this solution compare?
- What are the current approaches teams use today?
- Are there competing tools or solutions?
- What advantages does this solution provide over those alternatives?

## 9. Customer differentiators

- What differentiators would convince customers to accept this solution?
- What unique capabilities does it bring to client engagements?
- What potential barriers might customers raise?
- How can those barriers be mitigated?

## 10. Steady-state operational cost

- What is the steady-state operational cost?
- What infrastructure or licensing costs are involved?
- What ongoing operational effort is required?
- How does this cost compare to current delivery models?

## 11. Maintenance and evolution

- What investment is required to maintain and evolve the solution?
- What effort is needed for ongoing maintenance?
- How will the solution be supported and upgraded?
- What organizational ownership is required?

## 12. Delivery transformation

- How will this solution transform the service delivery model?
- How does it change the way services are delivered today?
- What new capabilities or efficiencies will it enable?
- How can this strengthen competitive differentiation in client engagements?
