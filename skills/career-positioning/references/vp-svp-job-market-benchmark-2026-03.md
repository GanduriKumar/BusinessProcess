## Common Skills

- Generative AI and Agentic AI fluency — now the most frequently cited requirement, with specific demand for agentic systems, agent frameworks (LangChain, LangGraph, AutoGPT), multi-agent coordination, and autonomous AI workflows
- AI governance, responsible AI, privacy, compliance, and regulatory understanding — explicitly required in financial services, life sciences, and enterprise IT roles; increasingly embedded in job descriptions as a design-time requirement, not a post-deployment afterthought
- AI commercialization, pricing, packaging, and go-to-market alignment — particularly prominent in consulting/advisory SVP roles (Dentsu/Merkle, AlixPartners) and enterprise SaaS roles
- Enterprise operating model and workflow redesign — roles consistently require experience moving organizations from pilots to production-scale AI operating models
- Executive communication and board-level storytelling — nearly every SVP role explicitly requires C-level engagement, board presentations, and the ability to translate complex AI concepts into business narratives
- Cross-functional influence across product, engineering, operations, risk, and business — every role requires bridging multiple functions; no pure-technology-silo roles at this level
- Architecture oversight for scalable AI platforms, data, MLOps / LLMOps — SmartRent, NatWest, State Street, CrowdStrike all require hands-on architecture judgment alongside strategic leadership
- Change management, enablement, and adoption leadership — explicit in Cytel, Dentsu/Merkle, and SmartRent roles
- AI opportunity framing tied to measurable ROI, EBITDA, cost, or growth outcomes — Dentsu/Merkle explicitly requires ROI models; CrowdStrike requires success metrics and value measurement
- Market analysis, partner ecosystem building, and vendor / platform evaluation — Dentsu/Merkle requires strategic partnerships with AI technology providers; Nokia requires standards body engagement
- Platform thinking for internal AI enablement, enterprise systems, and workflow productivity — SmartRent, BlackRock, and LaunchDarkly positions emphasize treating data and AI as internal products
- Client-facing solution framing for AI services, industry solutions, or professional services — explicit in Dentsu/Merkle (Fortune 500 clients), AlixPartners, and OpenText

## Common Experience Expectations

- 10+ to 15+ years of technology, operations, product, or transformation experience (SmartRent: 15–20 years; Dentsu/Merkle: 15+ years; CrowdStrike: 12+ years; Citi GenAI: 11–18 years)
- 2+ to 5+ years leading AI, automation, or digital transformation initiatives
- Proven delivery of production AI programs, not just pilots — explicitly stated in NatWest, Citi, CrowdStrike, SmartRent
- Experience translating AI capabilities into business-ready solutions with measurable outcomes
- Experience leading teams, shaping roadmaps, and setting architecture direction
- Experience influencing executive stakeholders and presenting to senior leadership or boards
- Experience driving adoption and organizational change in ambiguous, regulated, or complex environments
- In consulting/advisory roles, direct commercial experience in proposals, pitches, business development, pricing, and revenue generation
- In product-company roles, direct accountability for platform scale, enterprise systems, or customer-facing AI offerings
- Advanced degree preferred (MBA, MS, or PhD in Computer Science, Engineering, or related field) — explicitly mentioned in Nokia, Dentsu/Merkle, SmartRent, Cytel

## Common Role Expectations

- Set AI vision aligned to business goals and drive execution from strategy to production deployment
- Move from prototypes to secure, scalable, governed production systems
- Build or guide AI teams without losing business alignment — hire, mentor, develop, and retain top talent
- Define governance, safety, compliance, and responsible AI standards — increasingly a first-class role expectation, not a secondary concern
- Shape operating-model change, not just technical implementation — roles explicitly require organizational transformation alongside technology delivery
- Turn AI into measurable business value and strategic differentiation — ROI models, business cases, and value measurement explicitly expected
- Represent AI capability credibly to customers, partners, investors, boards, and internal leadership
- Stay current on tools, vendors, frameworks, and research, then convert that into actionable decisions
- In software product companies, connect AI capabilities to platform leverage, product advantage, and internal productivity
- In financial services, ensure AI solutions meet regulatory, audit, and trust requirements while maintaining production reliability

## Key Differences by Company Type

### Product companies (CrowdStrike, SmartRent, Pega, LaunchDarkly, Modern Health)
- Emphasis on platform-embedded AI, product-grade reliability, and customer-facing AI features
- Architecture ownership and hands-on technical depth expected alongside strategic leadership
- Success measured by platform adoption, reliability, and product differentiation

### Financial services (Citi, JPMorganChase, BNY, BlackRock, Barclays, State Street)
- Governance, regulatory compliance, audit, and risk management are non-negotiable first-class requirements
- Agentic AI adoption growing rapidly but within heavily governed environments
- VP/SVP titles are common for mid-to-senior leadership (VP at banks is not equivalent to VP at a product company)

### Consulting / professional services (Dentsu/Merkle, AlixPartners, OpenText)
- Revenue generation, business development, and client transformation are core expectations
- Billable utilization and practice-building leadership expected alongside thought leadership
- Enterprise platform integration expertise (Salesforce, Adobe, Microsoft, AWS, Google Cloud) is a differentiator

### Enterprise / internal IT (Nokia, NatWest Group, Accordion)
- Focus on internal transformation, IT modernization, and standards leadership
- Research-to-deployment pipeline management and cross-functional alignment
- Familiarity with regulatory and compliance frameworks within specific industry contexts

### Private equity / venture (Vista Equity Partners)
- AI applied to portfolio value creation and operational improvement across portfolio companies
- Commercial judgment and diligence experience valued alongside technical fluency

## Takeaway

- Senior AI roles in March 2026 are unmistakably converging on **Agentic AI** as the dominant capability theme — explicit "agentic" language now appears in role titles (CrowdStrike VP Agentic Systems, Mastercard VP Agentic Payments, Harri VP Agentic AI Platform) and job descriptions (Cytel, Dentsu/Merkle, State Street, Citi)
- **AI governance is now a design-time expectation**, not a compliance afterthought — roles require governance embedded in system design, not bolted on post-deployment (Cytel: "governance, validation, and risk management into system design"; NatWest: "familiarity with AI regulation and model governance framework")
- The market rewards leaders who combine **strategic business judgment with enough technical credibility** to guide architecture, governance, and adoption
- **Compensation at VP/SVP level is significant**: CrowdStrike $320K–$370K base + equity; Dentsu/Merkle competitive with performance bonus; SmartRent with equity participation
- Over the 90-day window, the demand pattern is accelerating: senior roles increasingly combine GenAI / Agentic AI fluency with commercialization, governance, operating-model design, executive influence, and **hands-on prototyping capability**
- The signal is clear: the market is not looking for abstract "AI strategy" leaders — it is looking for executives who can **make AI commercially useful, operationally governable, and organizationally adoptable** while maintaining enough technical depth to guide architecture and evaluate solutions firsthand
