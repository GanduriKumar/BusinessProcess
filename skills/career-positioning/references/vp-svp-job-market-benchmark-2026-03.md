## Common Skills

- Generative AI and Agentic AI fluency
- AI governance, responsible AI, privacy, and compliance understanding
- AI commercialization, pricing, packaging, and go-to-market alignment
- Enterprise operating model and workflow redesign
- Executive communication and board-level storytelling
- Cross-functional influence across product, engineering, operations, risk, and business
- Architecture oversight for scalable AI platforms, data, and MLOps / LLMOps
- Change management, enablement, and adoption leadership
- AI opportunity framing tied to measurable ROI, EBITDA, cost, or growth outcomes
- Market analysis, partner ecosystem building, and vendor / platform evaluation
- Platform thinking for internal AI enablement, enterprise systems, and workflow productivity
- Client-facing solution framing for AI services, industry solutions, or professional services

## Common Experience Expectations

- 10+ to 15+ years of technology, operations, product, or transformation experience
- 2+ to 5+ years leading AI, automation, or digital transformation initiatives
- Proven delivery of production AI programs, not just pilots
- Experience translating AI capabilities into business-ready solutions
- Experience leading teams, shaping roadmaps, and setting architecture direction
- Experience influencing executive stakeholders and presenting to senior leadership or boards
- Experience driving adoption and organizational change in ambiguous environments
- In some roles, direct commercial experience in proposals, diligence, pricing, and value realization
- In product-company roles, direct accountability for platform scale, enterprise systems, or customer-facing AI offerings

## Common Role Expectations

- Set AI vision aligned to business goals
- Move from prototypes to secure, scalable production deployment
- Build or guide AI teams without losing business alignment
- Define governance, safety, and usage standards
- Shape operating-model change, not just technical implementation
- Turn AI into measurable business value and strategic differentiation
- Represent AI capability credibly to customers, partners, investors, and internal leadership
- Stay current on tools, vendors, and research, then convert that into decisions
- In software product companies, connect AI capabilities to platform leverage, product advantage, and internal productivity

## Takeaway

- Senior AI roles are rewarding leaders who combine strategic business judgment with enough technical credibility to guide architecture, governance, and adoption.
- The market is not only looking for “AI experts.” It is looking for executives who can make AI commercially useful, operationally governable, and organizationally adoptable.
- Over a 90-day window, the demand pattern is consistent: senior roles increasingly combine GenAI / Agentic AI fluency with commercialization, governance, operating-model design, and executive influence.
- Across global tech and software product-company postings, the signal is clear: senior AI roles favor leaders who can bridge platform strategy, internal transformation, customer value, and executive trust.
